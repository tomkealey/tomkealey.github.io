I built a nice publishing pipeline… but also left a velvet rope in front of it. A quiet fear that if you publish something personal, imperfect, or experimental it might be judged. But here’s the thing: the best writing on the internet is written as if nobody is reading, so… Write posts in a way that you are the main audience. Date them so that they feel like journal entries. Publish “small and unimportant” on purpose – a paragraph, a note, a quote you like, a photo with one line of context. Looking “smart” online is boring. Looking human is memorable. Jot down one thing each day that is a “storyworthy” moments – odd encounters, tiny frustrations, small joy. You don’t have to make them polished essays. Just write them down. Treat it like a habit. It’s not about perfection, it’s about consistency. Start small, start simple, and start now. ...
